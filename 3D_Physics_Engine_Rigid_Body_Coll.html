3D Physics Engine: Rigid Bodies

Developed a physics engine to support rigid-body collision of 3D objects and integrated it into the existing in-house game engine, Azul. Created a demo showcasing free-falling chairs. Implemented various optimization techniques to enhance system performance.

My Work

Architected and Implemented collision system in 3 stages.

Collision Detection:

This stage is processed in two phases-broad phase and narrow phase. In the first phase (Broad), collision checks are performed between Bounding Volumes of game objects. I implemented techniques like Grid Spatial Partitioning and hash mapping which improved collision check efficiency by 20%. In the next phase (Narrow), colliding objects are traversed through their BVH to precisely find the point of contact.

Contact Generation:

In this stage, I implemented logic to generate collision data like interpenetration depth, contact normal, and coefficient of restitution based on the type and state of motion of the objects.

Contact Resolving:

Here, contacts are resolved by performing math operations and physics equations to get the final state of objects.

Further, I multithreaded the system, improving the performance by 12% and providing sufficient computational time for rendering and executing complex shaders.
